Website Evaluations

UNCC College of Arts + Architecture

This website is the homepage for the UNC Charlotte College of Arts + Architecture.

  • Contrast: The visibility of information is handled quite well with text being either white or black and the background being either white(body) or a dull green(header).
  • Repetition: The webpages are easily navigable and maintain consistent formatting.
  • Alignment: None of the pages are too busy, with sections of information being organized in a vertical orientation with the most relevant information at the top and subinformation following it further down the page.
  • Proximity: The information available is easy to navigate with clearly distinguishable links in text areas, formatting for event info, and easily accessible navigation menus to travel the website.

Yale School of Art

This is the "Ongoing Collaborative Experiment" being performed by the Yale School of Art(SoA) graduate school. It functions as the SoA homepage and as a wiki with the contributors being members of the SoA community.

  • Contrast: This varies from page to page on this site. On some of the pages, everything is perfectly legible but on others the overabundance of activity or color is nearly migraine inducing.
  • Repetition: While the page title and the header/footer information all have consistent formatting, that is almost the only thing consistent about the formatting between the pages.
  • Alignment: The best way I feel that I can describe this is visual vomit with a single island in the form of a vertical bar trying to hold it together.
  • Proximity: In contrast with the rest of the principles of CRAP demonstrated by this website, this has been done quite well. When looking through the information available, it is quite easy to find contact information, publishing information, and dates.
